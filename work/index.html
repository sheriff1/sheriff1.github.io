Product Designer

At AppFolio, a B2B property management software company, I work in teams of software engineers, quality assurance engineers, product managers, and agile coaches on customer-facing features using agile sprint development practices. Some of my duties include:

  • Scheduling and conducting customer interviews & usability tests
  • Distributing and analyzing surveys given tenants and property management professionals around improvements to current features and ideas for upcoming features
  • Developing and carrying out as design studios, risk assessments, and effort-value analyses with team members
  • Synthesizing ideas and concepts that arise from activities and customer inquiry into wireframes, prototypes, and mockups to be used to guide the development process
  • Providing insight towards the progression of our design system
  • Visiting customers to shadow them as they used various features of our application

Graduate Researcher

  • TaskAmbient
    For my masters thesis, I conducted research in understanding the impact of an ambient display used as a personal task management reference tool. The hypothesized value of using TaskAmbient were based on its visual format, ability to aggregate content across tools, its ability to delineate content based on user-defined context, and its ability to be instantly-on and readily available as opposed to having to navigate through individual applications to find task-based information.
    View case study on my TaskAmbient thesis work
  • During a summer program at Pacific Northwest National Laboratory, I conducted research in understanding two popular models of sensemaking in the data analytics community. Through user intereviews, I evaluated the two models across four domains of study known to perform data analysis with large-scale data.
    Publication: Toward a Deeper Understanding of Data Analysis, Sensemaking, and Signature Discovery
